<div class="subject">Further Maths</div> <div class="title">Gaussian Elimination</div> <br> <div class="date">10/01/24</div> <br><br><br> <div class="header">Gaussian Elimination: Solving Systems of Linear Equations</div> <div class="text">Gaussian elimination is a systematic method used to solve systems of linear equations by transforming an augmented matrix into row-echelon form and then back-substituting to find the solution. Let's explore the steps involved in Gaussian elimination.</div> <div class="header">1. Augmented Matrix</div> <div class="text">Consider a system of linear equations with \(m\) equations and \(n\) variables. Write the system in augmented matrix form \([A | \mathbf{B}]\), where \(A\) is the coefficient matrix, and \(\mathbf{B}\) is the column vector of constants.</div> <div class="header">2. Row-Echelon Form</div> <div class="text">Apply row operations to transform the augmented matrix into row-echelon form, where:</div> <div class="text">- The leading entry (pivot) in each row is 1.</div> <div class="text">- The leading entry of each subsequent row is to the right of the leading entry of the previous row.</div> <div class="text">- Rows with all zero entries are below rows with non-zero entries.</div> <div class="header">3. Back-Substitution</div> <div class="text">Start from the bottom row of the row-echelon form and back-substitute to find the values of the variables. Continue upward until all variables are determined.</div> <div class="header">4. Example</div> <div class="text">Let's consider a system of equations:</div> <div class="text">\[ \begin{align*} 2x + 3y - z &= 1 \\ 4x - 5y + 3z &= -2 \\ 6x - 9y + 5z &= 3 \end{align*} \]</div> <div class="text">The augmented matrix \([A | \mathbf{B}]\) is:</div> <div class="text">\[ \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 3 & -1 & | & 1 \\ 4 & -5 & 3 & | & -2 \\ 6 & -9 & 5 & | & 3 \end{bmatrix} \]</div> <div class="header">5. Applications</div> <div class="text">Gaussian elimination is widely used in solving systems of linear equations in fields such as physics, engineering, computer science, and economics.</div> <div class="header">Conclusion</div> <div class="text">Gaussian elimination provides a systematic approach to solving systems of linear equations, making it a valuable tool in various applications.

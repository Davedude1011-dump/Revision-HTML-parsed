<div class="subject">Further Maths</div> <div class="title">Calculating Determinants</div> <br> <div class="date">10/01/24</div> <br><br><br> <div class="header">Calculating Determinants of Matrices</div> <div class="text">The determinant of a matrix is a scalar value that provides important information about the properties of the matrix. Determinants find applications in various mathematical and scientific fields. Let's explore the methods for calculating determinants.</div> <div class="header">1. Determinant of a 2x2 Matrix</div> <div class="text">For a 2x2 matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} \), the determinant (\( \text{det}(A) \)) is calculated as:</div> <div class="text"> \[ \text{det}(A) = ad - bc \] </div> <div class="text">- **Example:** If \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 3 \\ -1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \), then \( \text{det}(A) = (2 \times 4) - (3 \times -1) = 11 \).</div> <div class="header">2. Determinant of a 3x3 Matrix</div> <div class="text">For a 3x3 matrix \( B = \begin{bmatrix} p & q & r \\ s & t & u \\ v & w & x \end{bmatrix} \), the determinant is calculated using the expansion by minors:</div> <div class="text"> \[ \text{det}(B) = p \times \text{det}(M_p) - q \times \text{det}(M_q) + r \times \text{det}(M_r) \] </div> <div class="text">where \( M_p, M_q, \) and \( M_r \) are 2x2 matrices obtained by removing the row and column of the respective element. The process is repeated for each element in the first row.</div> <div class="header">3. Determinant of a nxn Matrix</div> <div class="text">For a general nxn matrix \( C = [c_{ij}] \), the determinant is calculated using the Laplace expansion:</div> <div class="text"> \[ \text{det}(C) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} (-1)^{i+j} \times c_{ij} \times \text{det}(M_{ij}) \] </div> <div class="text">where \( M_{ij} \) is the (n-1)x(n-1) matrix obtained by removing the ith row and jth column.</div> <div class="header">4. Properties of Determinants</div> <div class="text">- The determinant of a matrix and its transpose are equal: \( \text{det}(A^T) = \text{det}(A) \).</div> <div class="text">- If a matrix has a row or column of zeros, its determinant is zero.</div> <div class="text">- Multiplying a row or column of a matrix by a scalar multiplies the determinant by that scalar.</div> <div class="header">5. Applications</div> <div class="text">- Solving systems of linear equations using Cramer's Rule, where determinants are used to find the coefficients of the variables.</div> <div class="text">- Analyzing invertibility: A matrix is invertible if and only if its determinant is non-zero.</div> <div class="header">Conclusion</div> <div class="text">Determinants are valuable tools in linear algebra with applications ranging from solving equations to determining the invertibility of matrices.

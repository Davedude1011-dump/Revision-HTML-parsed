<div class="subject">Further Maths</div> <div class="title">Properties of Determinants</div> <br> <div class="date">10/01/24</div> <br><br><br> <div class="header">Properties of Determinants</div> <div class="text">Determinants possess several properties that are crucial for understanding their behavior and applications in linear algebra. These properties contribute to simplifying calculations and establishing relationships between matrices. Let's explore the key properties of determinants.</div> <div class="header">1. Effect of Row Operations</div> <div class="text">Performing elementary row operations on a matrix affects its determinant as follows:</div> <div class="text">- Multiplying a row by a scalar \( c \) multiplies the determinant by \( c \).</div> <div class="text">- Swapping two rows negates the determinant.</div> <div class="text">- Adding a multiple of one row to another row leaves the determinant unchanged.</div> <div class="header">2. Effect of Elementary Matrices</div> <div class="text">An elementary matrix is obtained by applying a single elementary row operation to an identity matrix. The determinant of an elementary matrix is easy to determine:</div> <div class="text">- The determinant of an elementary matrix is the same as the scalar used in the row operation.</div> <div class="header">3. Determinant of a Product</div> <div class="text">For two matrices \( A \) and \( B \), the determinant of their product \( AB \) is equal to the product of their determinants:</div> <div class="text">\[ \text{det}(AB) = \text{det}(A) \times \text{det}(B) \]</div> <div class="header">4. Determinant of the Inverse</div> <div class="text">The determinant of the inverse of a square matrix \( A^{-1} \) is the reciprocal of the determinant of \( A \):</div> <div class="text">\[ \text{det}(A^{-1}) = \frac{1}{\text{det}(A)} \]</div> <div class="header">5. Determinant of the Transpose</div> <div class="text">The determinant of the transpose of a matrix \( A^T \) is the same as the determinant of \( A \):</div> <div class="text">\[ \text{det}(A^T) = \text{det}(A) \]</div> <div class="header">6. Determinant of a Block Matrix</div> <div class="text">For a block matrix with square submatrices along the diagonal, the determinant is the product of the determinants of the individual submatrices:</div> <div class="text">\[ \text{det}\begin{pmatrix} A & B \\ 0 & C \end{pmatrix} = \text{det}(A) \times \text{det}(C) \]</div> <div class="header">7. Determinant of Upper or Lower Triangular Matrix</div> <div class="text">The determinant of an upper or lower triangular matrix is the product of its diagonal elements:</div> <div class="text">\[ \text{det}\begin{bmatrix} a_{11} & a_{12} & \cdots & a_{1n} \\ 0 & a_{22} & \cdots & a_{2n} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & \cdots & a_{nn} \end{bmatrix} = a_{11} \times a_{22} \times \cdots \times a_{nn} \]</div> <div class="header">Conclusion</div> <div class="text">Understanding the properties of determinants is essential for efficiently manipulating matrices and solving systems of linear equations.

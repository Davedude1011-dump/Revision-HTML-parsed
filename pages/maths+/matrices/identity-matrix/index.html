It serves a fundamental role in linear algebra and various mathematical operations. Let's explore the definition and properties of the identity matrix.</div> <div class="header">1. Definition</div> <div class="text">The identity matrix, denoted as \(I\), is a square matrix with ones on the main diagonal and zeros elsewhere. For an \(n \times n\) identity matrix:</div> <div class="text">\[ I_n = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & \cdots & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & \cdots & 1 \end{bmatrix} \]</div> <div class="header">2. Properties</div> <div class="text">- **Multiplicative Identity:** For any matrix \(A\) of appropriate size, \(AI = IA = A\).</div> <div class="text">- **Scalar Multiplication:** Multiplying the identity matrix by a scalar \(c\) yields the identity matrix scaled by \(c\): \(cI = Ic = cI_n\).</div> <div class="text">- **Matrix Addition:** Adding the identity matrix to any matrix \(A\) leaves \(A\) unchanged: \(A + I = I + A = A\).</div> <div class="header">3. Role in Matrix Multiplication</div> <div class="text">The identity matrix acts as the multiplicative identity in matrix multiplication. For a matrix \(A\) of size \(m \times n\), \(AI = IA = A\).</div> <div class="header">4. Application in Linear Algebra</div> <div class="text">The identity matrix is a fundamental concept in linear algebra, used in defining inverses, solving systems of linear equations, and various matrix operations.</div> <div class="header">5. Example</div> <div class="text">Consider a 3x3 identity matrix \(I_3\):</div> <div class="text">\[ I_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \]</div> <div class="header">Conclusion</div> <div class="text">The identity matrix is a key element in matrix algebra, providing a foundational tool for various mathematical operations.

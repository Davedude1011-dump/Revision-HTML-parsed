Understanding these properties enhances the manipulation and analysis of matrices. Let's explore the key properties of matrix inverses.</div> <div class="header">1. Existence and Uniqueness</div> <div class="text">If a square matrix \(A\) is invertible (non-singular), it has a unique inverse denoted as \(A^{-1}\). The existence of an inverse is determined by the non-zero determinant of \(A\).</div> <div class="header">2. Multiplicative Property</div> <div class="text">For two invertible matrices \(A\) and \(B\), the product \(AB\) is also invertible, and \((AB)^{-1} = B^{-1}A^{-1}\).</div> <div class="header">3. Inverse of a Product</div> <div class="text">If a product \(AB\) is invertible, then both \(A\) and \(B\) are invertible. The inverse of the product is given by \((AB)^{-1} = B^{-1}A^{-1}\).</div> <div class="header">4. Inverse of an Inverse</div> <div class="text">The inverse of the inverse of a matrix \(A\) is the matrix \(A\) itself: \((A^{-1})^{-1} = A\).</div> <div class="header">5. Transposition of an Inverse</div> <div class="text">The transpose of the inverse of a matrix \(A\) is the inverse of the transpose: \((A^T)^{-1} = (A^{-1})^T\).</div> <div class="header">6. Scalar Multiplication</div> <div class="text">For any non-zero scalar \(c\), the inverse of the scalar multiple \(cA\) is given by \((cA)^{-1} = \frac{1}{c}A^{-1}\).</div> <div class="header">7. Identity Matrix</div> <div class="text">The identity matrix serves as the multiplicative identity for matrix multiplication. For any invertible matrix \(A\), the product \(AI = IA = A\).</div> <div class="header">8. Homogeneous System of Equations</div> <div class="text">For a homogeneous system of linear equations \(AX = \mathbf{0}\), where \(\mathbf{0}\) is the zero vector, the unique solution is the trivial solution \(\mathbf{0}\) if and only if the coefficient matrix \(A\) is invertible.</div> <div class="header">Conclusion</div> <div class="text">Understanding the properties of matrix inverses is essential for efficient manipulation of matrices and solving systems of linear equations.

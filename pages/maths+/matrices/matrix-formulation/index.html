These operations follow specific rules and properties, making matrix algebra a powerful tool for solving systems of linear equations and representing transformations.</div> <div class="header">3. Matrix Multiplication</div> <div class="text">Matrix multiplication involves the product of rows and columns. If matrix \(A\) is of size \(m \times p\) and matrix \(B\) is of size \(p \times n\), their product \(C = AB\) is a matrix of size \(m \times n\). The entry \(C_{ij}\) is the dot product of the \(i\)-th row of \(A\) and the \(j\)-th column of \(B\).</div> <div class="header">4.

The rules for these operations are based on the corresponding elements of the matrices involved.</div> <div class="header">1. Matrix Addition</div> <div class="text">- **Definition:** Given two matrices, A and B, of the same order (having the same number of rows and columns), their sum, denoted as A + B, is obtained by adding corresponding elements:</div> <div class="text"> \[ (A + B)_{ij} = A_{ij} + B_{ij} \] </div> <div class="text">- **Example:** If \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( B = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 \\ 7 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \), then \( A + B = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 8 \\ 10 & 12 \end{bmatrix} \).</div> <div class="header">2. Matrix Subtraction</div> <div class="text">- **Definition:** The difference of matrices A and B, denoted as A - B, is obtained by subtracting corresponding elements:</div> <div class="text"> \[ (A - B)_{ij} = A_{ij} - B_{ij} \] </div> <div class="text">- **Example:** Using the matrices A and B from the previous example, \( A - B = \begin{bmatrix} -4 & -4 \\ -4 & -4 \end{bmatrix} \).</div> <div class="header">3. Properties of Matrix Addition and Subtraction</div> <div class="text">- **Commutative Property:** Matrix addition is commutative, meaning that \( A + B = B + A \).</div> <div class="text">- **Associative Property:** Matrix addition is associative, meaning that \( (A + B) + C = A + (B + C) \).</div> <div class="text">- **Inverse Elements:** For any matrix A, there exists an additive inverse (-A) such that \( A + (-A) = 0 \), where 0 is the zero matrix.</div> <div class="header">4. Applications</div> <div class="text">- **Linear Transformations:** Matrices are used to represent linear transformations, and addition/subtraction play a role in combining or modifying these transformations.</div> <div class="text">- **Systems of Linear Equations:** Matrix operations are fundamental in solving systems of linear equations, where addition and subtraction are key steps in the process.</div> <div class="header">Conclusion</div> <div class="text">Matrix addition and subtraction provide essential tools for manipulating and combining linear transformations and solving systems of linear equations.

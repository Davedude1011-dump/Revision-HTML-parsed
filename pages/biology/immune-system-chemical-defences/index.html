Complement System</div> <div class="text">- **Complement Proteins:** A group of proteins that complement the action of antibodies and play roles in inflammation, opsonization (marking pathogens for phagocytosis), and the formation of membrane attack complexes for direct pathogen destruction.</div> <div class="text">- **Activation Pathways:** The complement system can be activated through classical, lectin, or alternative pathways, providing a rapid and versatile defense mechanism.</div> <div class="header">3. Interferons</div> <div class="text">- **Type I Interferons:** Released in response to viral infections, these proteins inhibit viral replication within host cells and enhance the immune response against viruses.</div> <div class="text">- **Type II Interferon (IFN-Î³):** Activates immune cells, promotes antiviral activity, and enhances the overall immune response.</div> <div class="header">4. Cytokines</div> <div class="text">- **Signaling Molecules:** Cytokines are signaling proteins that regulate immune responses. Interleukins, for example, coordinate communication between immune cells, while chemokines attract immune cells to specific sites of infection or inflammation.</div> <div class="text">- **Tumor Necrosis Factor (TNF):** Promotes inflammation and helps control infections, playing a crucial role in the immune response.</div> <div class="header">5. Acidic pH</div> <div class="text">- **Stomach Acidity:** The low pH of the stomach, maintained by gastric acid, not only aids in digestion but also creates an environment that hinders the growth of ingested pathogens.</div> <div class="text">- **Vaginal Acidity:** The acidic pH of the vagina contributes to preventing the overgrowth of harmful bacteria and yeast.</div> <div class="header">Conclusion</div> <div class="text">Chemical defenses, including antimicrobial proteins, the complement system, interferons, cytokines, and acidic environments, enhance the immune system's ability to recognize, neutralize, and eliminate pathogens.

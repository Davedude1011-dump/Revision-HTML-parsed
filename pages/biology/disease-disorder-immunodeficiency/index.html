Causes of Immunodeficiency</div> <div class="text">- **Genetic Factors:** Inherited mutations affecting immune system components can lead to primary immunodeficiency disorders.</div> <div class="text">- **Infections:** Chronic or severe infections, especially those affecting immune cells, can contribute to secondary immunodeficiency.</div> <div class="text">- **Medical Treatments:** Certain medical treatments, such as chemotherapy or organ transplantation, may suppress the immune system, leading to immunodeficiency.</div> <div class="header">4. Clinical Manifestations</div> <div class="text">- **Increased Susceptibility:** Individuals with immunodeficiency disorders are more prone to recurrent or severe infections, often affecting the respiratory, gastrointestinal, or urinary systems.</div> <div class="text">- **Delayed Healing:** Wounds and injuries may take longer to heal in individuals with compromised immune function.</div> <div class="header">5. Diagnosis and Management</div> <div class="text">- **Diagnostic Tests:** Immunological tests, genetic screenings, and assessments of immune cell function help diagnose immunodeficiency disorders.</div> <div class="text">- **Treatment Approaches:** Management involves addressing underlying causes, administering immunoglobulin replacement therapy, and, in some cases, bone marrow or stem cell transplantation.</div> <div class="header">6.

<div class="subject">Biology</div> <div class="title">Immune System</div> <br> <div class="date">09/01/24</div> <br><br><br> <div class="header">Immune System: Overview</div> <div class="text">The immune system is a complex network of cells, tissues, and organs that work together to defend the body against harmful invaders, including pathogens, toxins, and abnormal cells. Its primary functions involve recognizing and eliminating threats while maintaining tolerance to the body's own cells. Let's explore the key components and mechanisms of the immune system:</div> <div class="header">1. Components of the Immune System</div> <div class="text">- **White Blood Cells (Leukocytes):** These cells are crucial for immune responses and are divided into two main types: phagocytes, which engulf and digest pathogens, and lymphocytes, which include B cells and T cells that play central roles in adaptive immunity.</div> <div class="text">- **Antibodies:** Proteins produced by B cells that recognize and neutralize specific pathogens. Antibodies are a key component of the humoral immune response.</div> <div class="text">- **Complement System:** A group of proteins that enhance the immune response by promoting the destruction of pathogens, inflammation, and the recognition of foreign substances.</div> <div class="text">- **Lymphatic System:** A network of vessels and organs, including lymph nodes and the spleen, that transport lymph (fluid containing immune cells) and facilitate immune responses.</div> <div class="header">2. Innate Immunity</div> <div class="text">- **Physical Barriers:** Skin and mucous membranes act as physical barriers, preventing pathogens from entering the body.</div> <div class="text">- **Phagocytic Cells:** Cells like macrophages and neutrophils engulf and digest pathogens.</div> <div class="text">- **Inflammation:** A localized response to injury or infection that enhances immune cell activity and promotes healing.</div> <div class="header">3. Adaptive Immunity</div> <div class="text">- **Antigen Recognition:** B cells and T cells recognize specific antigens, which are unique molecular markers on pathogens.</div> <div class="text">- **B Cells:** Produce antibodies that target and neutralize pathogens.</div> <div class="text">- **T Cells:** Coordinate immune responses and directly attack infected or abnormal cells.</div> <div class="text">- **Memory Cells:** Long-lived cells that "remember" previous encounters with specific pathogens, providing a quicker and more robust response upon re-exposure.</div> <div class="header">4. Immunization</div> <div class="text">- **Vaccination:** The introduction of harmless forms of pathogens or their components to stimulate an immune response and develop immunity without causing the disease.</div> <div class="text">- **Herd Immunity:** When a significant portion of a population becomes immune to a disease, it provides indirect protection to those who are not immune.</div> <div class="header">5. Immune Disorders</div> <div class="text">- **Autoimmune Diseases:** Conditions where the immune system mistakenly attacks the body's own cells and tissues, such as rheumatoid arthritis and lupus.</div> <div class="text">- **Immunodeficiency:** Conditions characterized by a weakened or absent immune response, as seen in HIV/AIDS.</div> <div class="header">Conclusion</div> <div class="text">The immune system is a vital defense mechanism that protects the body from infections and maintains overall health.

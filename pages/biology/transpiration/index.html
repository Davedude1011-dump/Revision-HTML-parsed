This process is driven by various factors, including sunlight, temperature, humidity, and wind. As water evaporates from the leaf surface, it creates a negative pressure that draws more water from the roots.</div> <div class="header">2. **Stomatal Control and Regulation**</div> <div class="text">Stomata, tiny pores on the leaf surface, play a pivotal role in transpiration. The opening and closing of stomata are regulated by guard cells. This control mechanism helps plants manage water loss and carbon dioxide uptake, ensuring a balance between water conservation and gas exchange.</div> <div class="header">3. **Functions of Transpiration**</div> <div class="text">Transpiration serves several essential functions in plants. It facilitates the upward movement of water and nutrients from the roots to the leaves through the xylem vessels.

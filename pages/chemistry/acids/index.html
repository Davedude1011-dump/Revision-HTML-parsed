They are commonly categorized as either strong or weak acids based on their ionization in water and ability to donate protons (H+ ions).</div> <div class="header">Strong Acids</div> <div class="text">1. **Definition:** Strong acids completely ionize in water, releasing a high concentration of hydrogen ions (H+).</div> <div class="text">2. **Examples of Strong Acids:** Hydrochloric acid (HCl), sulfuric acid (H2SO4), and nitric acid (HNO3) are classic examples of strong acids.</div> <div class="text">3. **Ionization Process:** The ionization equation for strong acids is written without equilibrium arrows, indicating complete dissociation. For example, HCl (g) → H+ (aq) + Cl- (aq).</div> <div class="text">4. **Conductivity:** Due to their complete ionization, strong acids are excellent conductors of electricity when dissolved in water.</div> <div class="header">Weak Acids</div> <div class="text">1. **Definition:** Weak acids partially ionize in water, resulting in a limited release of hydrogen ions.</div> <div class="text">2. **Examples of Weak Acids:** Acetic acid (CH3COOH) and citric acid (found in citrus fruits) are common examples of weak acids.</div> <div class="text">3. **Ionization Process:** The ionization equation for weak acids includes equilibrium arrows, indicating an incomplete dissociation. For example, CH3COOH (aq) ⇌ H+ (aq) + CH3COO- (aq).</div> <div class="text">4. **Conductivity:** Weak acids exhibit lower conductivity in comparison to strong acids due to their partial ionization.</div> <div class="header">Characteristics of Acids</div> <div class="text">1. **Sour Taste:** Many acids have a sour taste, such as the tartness of citrus fruits.</div> <div class="text">2. **Color Changes:** Acids can change the color of certain indicators. For example, litmus paper turns red in the presence of an acid.</div> <div class="text">3. **Corrosive Nature:** Strong acids can be corrosive and react with metals, producing hydrogen gas.</div> <div class="text">4. **pH Levels:** Acids have pH values below 7, with lower pH values indicating stronger acidity.</div> <div class="text">Understanding the distinction between strong and weak acids is essential for various applications, including the design of chemical processes and the understanding of their impact on the environment.</div> <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gYBbzkqrmE" class="link">video recource (1/4)</a><br> <iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/_gYBbzkqrmE?si=nooqQiEbJQAc0aEx" title="YouTube video player" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer;

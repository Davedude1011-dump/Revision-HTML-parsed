Oxidation Reactions</div> <div class="text">Oxidation is a chemical process in which a substance loses electrons. It is often associated with an increase in oxidation state or a gain in oxygen. Common examples include the rusting of iron and combustion reactions. In oxidation, there is a transfer of electrons from the substance being oxidized to another substance, typically the oxidizing agent.</div> <div class="header">2. Oxidation Numbers</div> <div class="text">Oxidation numbers, also known as oxidation states, are assigned to atoms to track the distribution of electrons in a compound or ion. Rules govern the assignment of oxidation numbers, such as assigning +1 to hydrogen and -2 to oxygen in most compounds. The sum of oxidation numbers in a neutral compound is zero, while in ions, it equals the ion's charge.</div> <div class="header">3. Rules for Assigning Oxidation Numbers</div> <div class="text">a. In a neutral compound, the sum of oxidation numbers is zero. <br> b. In ions, the sum of oxidation numbers equals the charge of the ion. <br> c. Hydrogen typically has an oxidation number of +1. <br> d. Oxygen usually has an oxidation number of -2. <br> e. Fluorine is assigned an oxidation number of -1 in compounds. <br> f. Group 1 metals (alkali metals) have an oxidation number of +1. <br> g. Group 2 metals (alkaline earth metals) have an oxidation number of +2.</div> <div class="header">4. Examples of Oxidation Reactions</div> <div class="text"> a. **Combustion of Methane:** CH<sub>4</sub> + 2O<sub>2</sub> → CO<sub>2</sub> + 2H<sub>2</sub>O <br> b. **Rusting of Iron:** 4Fe + 3O<sub>2</sub> → 2Fe<sub>2</sub>O<sub>3</sub> </div> <div class="header">5. Significance of Oxidation Numbers</div> <div class="text">Oxidation numbers provide a systematic way to track the electron transfer in chemical reactions. They help in balancing redox reactions, where both oxidation and reduction occur simultaneously. Understanding oxidation reactions and oxidation numbers is crucial in various fields, including analytical chemistry, biochemistry, and industrial processes.</div> <div class="header">Conclusion</div> <div class="text">Oxidation reactions, involving the loss of electrons, and oxidation numbers, indicating the electron distribution in compounds, are fundamental concepts in chemistry.

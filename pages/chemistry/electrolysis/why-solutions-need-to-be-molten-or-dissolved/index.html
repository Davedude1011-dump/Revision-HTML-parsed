This conductivity is essential for the flow of current between the anode and cathode, ensuring efficient redox reactions.</div> <div class="header">3. Redox Reactions in Solution</div> <div class="text">- **Solubility of Reactants:** Many substances undergo electrolysis more readily when in a dissolved state. The solubility of reactants ensures a higher concentration of ions in the solution, promoting a more effective electrochemical process.</div> <div class="header">4. Reduction of Operating Temperature</div> <div class="text">- **Molten State:** In some cases, substances need to be in a molten state for electrolysis to occur. This reduces the operating temperature, making the process more practical for certain applications, such as the extraction of metals.</div> <div class="header">5. Minimization of Side Reactions</div> <div class="text">- **Purity of Electrolyte:** Molten or dissolved states allow for better control over the purity of the electrolyte.

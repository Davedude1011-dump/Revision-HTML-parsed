<div class="subject">Chemistry</div> <div class="title">Specific Setups</div> <br> <div class="date">12/01/24</div> <br><br><br> <div class="header">Electrolysis Setups for Common Electrolytes</div> <div class="text">Electrolysis, a process driven by the application of an electric current, finds varied applications in industry and research. Different electrolytes yield distinct products, and here we explore specific electrolysis setups for common electrolytes, shedding light on the transformations occurring at the anode and cathode.</div> <div class="header">1. Water Electrolysis</div> <div class="text">- **Setup:** In a water electrolysis setup, a container with water (H₂O) is equipped with two electrodes—usually graphite or platinum—an anode and a cathode.</div> <div class="text">- **Anode Reaction:** At the anode, water undergoes oxidation, releasing oxygen gas: \(2H₂O(l) \rightarrow O₂(g) + 4H^+(aq) + 4e^-\).</div> <div class="text">- **Cathode Reaction:** At the cathode, reduction of water produces hydrogen gas: \(4H₂O(l) + 4e^- \rightarrow 2H₂(g) + 4OH^-(aq)\).</div> <div class="header">2. Copper(II) Sulfate Electrolysis</div> <div class="text">- **Setup:** A copper(II) sulfate (CuSO₄) solution serves as the electrolyte. Copper electrodes are commonly used.</div> <div class="text">- **Anode Reaction:** Copper anode dissolution occurs: \(Cu(s) \rightarrow Cu^{2+}(aq) + 2e^-\).</div> <div class="text">- **Cathode Reaction:** Copper ions in solution are reduced at the cathode, depositing copper: \(Cu^{2+}(aq) + 2e^- \rightarrow Cu(s)\).</div> <div class="header">3. Sodium Chloride Electrolysis (Chlor-Alkali Process)</div> <div class="text">- **Setup:** Electrolysis of a sodium chloride (NaCl) solution, often referred to as the chlor-alkali process, involves a diaphragm cell or a mercury cell.</div> <div class="text">- **Anode Reaction:** Chlorine gas is produced at the anode: \(2Cl^-(aq) \rightarrow Cl₂(g) + 2e^-\).</div> <div class="text">- **Cathode Reaction:** Sodium ions are reduced at the cathode, forming sodium hydroxide and hydrogen gas: \(2H₂O(l) + 2e^- \rightarrow H₂(g) + 2OH^-(aq)\).</div> <div class="header">4. Sulfuric Acid Electrolysis</div> <div class="text">- **Setup:** An electrolyte of sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄) with graphite or platinum electrodes is commonly employed.</div> <div class="text">- **Anode Reaction:** At the anode, water is oxidized, producing oxygen gas: \(4H₂O(l) \rightarrow O₂(g) + 4H^+(aq) + 4e^-\).</div> <div class="text">- **Cathode Reaction:** Hydrogen ions are reduced at the cathode, forming hydrogen gas: \(4H^+(aq) + 4e^- \rightarrow 2H₂(g)\).</div> <div class="header">5. Aluminum Production</div> <div class="text">- **Setup:** In the Hall-Héroult process for aluminum production, aluminum oxide (Al₂O₃) dissolved in molten cryolite serves as the electrolyte with carbon anodes and cathodes made of graphite.</div> <div class="text">- **Anode Reaction:** Oxygen is produced at the carbon anode: \(2O^{2-}(l) \rightarrow O₂(g) + 4e^-\).</div> <div class="text">- **Cathode Reaction:** Aluminum ions are reduced at the cathode, forming liquid aluminum: \(Al^{3+}(l) + 3e^- \rightarrow Al(l)\).</div> <div class="header">Conclusion</div> <div class="text">These electrolysis setups showcase the versatility of the process, offering a glimpse into the transformations occurring at the anode and cathode for various common electrolytes.

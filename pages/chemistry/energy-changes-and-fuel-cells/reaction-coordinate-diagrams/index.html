It depicts the progression of a reaction from the starting materials (reactants) to the final products along the reaction pathway.</div> <div class="header">Components of a Reaction Coordinate Diagram</div> <div class="text">- **Reactants:** The starting materials of the reaction, represented on the left side of the diagram.</div> <div class="text">- **Products:** The final products of the reaction, represented on the right side of the diagram.</div> <div class="text">- **Transition State:** The highest energy point along the reaction pathway, also known as the activated complex. It represents the point of maximum energy barrier that must be overcome for the reaction to proceed.</div> <div class="text">- **Intermediate Species:** Any species formed temporarily during the reaction that is neither a reactant nor a product.</div> <div class="header">Energy Profile</div> <div class="text">The reaction coordinate diagram plots the energy of the system as a function of the reaction progress. The vertical axis represents the energy (usually in kilojoules per mole), while the horizontal axis represents the progress of the reaction from reactants to products.</div> <div class="header">Key Features</div> <div class="text">- **Activation Energy:** The energy difference between the reactants and the transition state, representing the energy barrier that must be overcome for the reaction to occur.</div> <div class="text">- **Reaction Energy:** The overall energy change (enthalpy change) of the reaction, calculated as the difference in energy between the products and the reactants.</div> <div class="text">- **Exothermic vs. Endothermic:** Depending on the relative energies of the reactants and products, the reaction coordinate diagram can show whether the reaction is exothermic (releases energy) or endothermic (absorbs energy).</div> <div class="header">Applications</div> <div class="text">Reaction coordinate diagrams are widely used in chemistry to understand reaction mechanisms, predict reaction rates, and analyze the thermodynamics of chemical reactions.

These reactions are facilitated by catalysts and occur in an electrolyte medium. Here's an overview of the electrochemical reactions in fuel cells:</div> <div class="header">1. Anode Reaction</div> <div class="text">- **Oxidation:** At the anode, the fuel undergoes oxidation, typically releasing electrons and protons.</div> <div class="text">- **Example (Hydrogen Fuel Cell):** For a hydrogen fuel cell, the anode reaction is:</div> <div class="text">H₂ ⟶ 2H⁺ + 2e⁻</div> <div class="text">- **Hydrogen Oxidation:** Hydrogen molecules dissociate into protons and electrons in the presence of a catalyst, commonly platinum.</div> <div class="header">2. Cathode Reaction</div> <div class="text">- **Reduction:** At the cathode, the oxidant (typically oxygen or air) undergoes reduction, usually by combining with electrons and protons.</div> <div class="text">- **Example (Hydrogen Fuel Cell):** The cathode reaction for a hydrogen fuel cell is:</div> <div class="text">O₂ + 4H⁺ + 4e⁻ ⟶ 2H₂O</div> <div class="text">- **Oxygen Reduction:** Oxygen molecules from the air react with protons and electrons to form water.</div> <div class="header">3. Overall Reaction</div> <div class="text">- **Electrochemical Process:** The overall electrochemical reaction in a hydrogen fuel cell combines the anode and cathode reactions:</div> <div class="text">H₂ + ½O₂ ⟶ H₂O</div> <div class="text">- **Net Reaction:** Hydrogen fuel combines with oxygen to produce water, releasing energy that is harnessed as electrical power.</div> <div class="header">4. Catalysts and Electrolyte</div> <div class="text">- **Catalysts:** Catalysts, such as platinum, are used to facilitate the electrochemical reactions at the electrodes, reducing activation energy and improving reaction kinetics.</div> <div class="text">- **Electrolyte:** The electrolyte medium allows for the transport of ions (e.g., protons) between the anode and cathode, enabling the completion of the electrochemical circuit.</div> <div class="header">5. Efficiency and Applications</div> <div class="text">- **Efficiency:** Fuel cells offer high efficiency in converting chemical energy into electrical energy, making them attractive for various applications.</div> <div class="text">- **Applications:** Fuel cells are used in transportation (e.g., hydrogen fuel cell vehicles), stationary power generation, portable electronics, and other fields where clean and efficient energy is desired.</div> <div class="header">Conclusion</div> <div class="text">Electrochemical reactions in fuel cells play a pivotal role in generating electrical energy from chemical fuels with high efficiency and low environmental impact.

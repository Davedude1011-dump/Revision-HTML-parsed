Reaction Kinetics</div> <div class="text">- **Rate of Reaction:** Activation energy influences the rate at which a chemical reaction proceeds. Reactions with higher activation energies typically occur at slower rates, while those with lower activation energies proceed more rapidly.</div> <div class="text">- **Collision Theory:** According to collision theory, molecules must collide with sufficient energy and proper orientation to react and form products. The activation energy represents the threshold energy required for effective collisions to occur.</div> <div class="header">2. Factors Affecting Activation Energy</div> <div class="text">- **Nature of Reactants:** Different chemical reactions exhibit varying activation energies based on the types of bonds broken and formed during the process. Reactions involving stronger bonds typically have higher activation energies.</div> <div class="text">- **Temperature:** Increasing the temperature of a reaction system generally decreases the activation energy by providing reactant molecules with greater kinetic energy. This enhances the likelihood of successful collisions and accelerates the reaction rate.</div> <div class="text">- **Catalysts:** Catalysts lower the activation energy by providing an alternative reaction pathway with a lower energy barrier. They facilitate the reaction without being consumed, thereby increasing the rate of reaction.</div> <div class="header">3. Potential Energy Diagram</div> <div class="text">- **Energy Profile:** Activation energy is depicted on a potential energy diagram, which illustrates the energy changes during a chemical reaction. The activation energy corresponds to the difference in energy between the reactants' initial state and the transition state.</div> <div class="text">- **Transition State:** The transition state, also known as the activated complex, represents the highest energy point along the reaction pathway. Molecules at this state have partially formed and partially broken bonds.</div> <div class="header">4.

Segment Area Formula</div> <div class="text">- **Segment Definition:** A segment is the region bounded by a chord and the arc it subtends.</div> <div class="text">- **Formula:** The area (\(A_{seg}\)) of a segment with central angle \(n\) in degrees and radius \(r\) is given by \(A_{seg} = \frac{1}{2} r^2 \left(\frac{n}{360} \pi - \sin\frac{n}{2}\right)\).</div> <div class="text">- **Explanation:** The formula combines the area of a sector with the area of a triangle formed by the radius and the chord.</div> <div class="header">2. Finding Central Angle</div> <div class="text">- **Central Angle Formula:** To find the central angle \(n\) when given the segment area \(A_{seg}\) and radius \(r\), rearrange the segment area formula as \(n = \arcsin\left(1 - \frac{2A_{seg}}{\pi r^2}\right) \times 2\).</div> <div class="text">- **Application:** This variation is useful when you have the segment area and need to determine the central angle.</div> <div class="header">3. Finding Radius</div> <div class="text">- **Radius Formula:** If the segment area \(A_{seg}\) and central angle \(n\) are known, the radius \(r\) can be found using \(r = \sqrt{\frac{2A_{seg}}{\frac{\pi}{180} n - \sin n}}\).</div> <div class="text">- **Purpose:** This formula is handy when you have the segment area and central angle but need to find the radius.</div> <div class="header">4.

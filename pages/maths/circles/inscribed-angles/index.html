Definition and Formation</div> <div class="text">- **Definition:** An inscribed angle is an angle formed by two intersecting chords or a chord and a tangent in a circle. The vertex of the angle is on the circle's circumference.</div> <div class="text">- **Formation:** Inscribed angles are created by drawing lines from the circle's center to the endpoints of the chord or by incorporating tangents.</div> <div class="header">2. Properties of Inscribed Angles</div> <div class="text">- **Central Angle Relationship:** An inscribed angle is equal to the central angle subtended by the same arc, sharing the same endpoints on the circle.</div> <div class="text">- **Half the Measure of Intercepted Arc:** The measure of an inscribed angle is half the measure of the intercepted arc between its sides.</div> <div class="text">- **Congruence:** Inscribed angles subtending the same or congruent arcs are themselves congruent.</div> <div class="header">3. Angle Measures and Intercepted Arcs</div> <div class="text">- **Formula:** The measure of an inscribed angle (∠A) is given by the formula ∠A = 0.5 * m(arc BC), where m(arc BC) is the measure of the intercepted arc BC.</div> <div class="text">- **Applications:** This formula is applicable in determining angle measures when the intercepted arcs are known.</div> <div class="header">4. Real-World Applications</div> <div class="text">- **Surveying:** Inscribed angles play a role in land surveying, where they can be used to measure distances and angles between points.</div> <div class="text">- **Design and Architecture:** Understanding inscribed angles is valuable in designing circular structures, ensuring accurate and aesthetically pleasing layouts.</div> <div class="header">5.

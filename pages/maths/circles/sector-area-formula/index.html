Let's explore the sector area formula and its variations, always using degrees as specified.</div> <div class="header">1. Sector Area Formula</div> <div class="text">- **Formula:** The area (A) of a sector is calculated using the formula \( A = \frac{n}{360} \pi r^2 \), where \( n \) is the central angle in degrees, \( \pi \) is the mathematical constant pi (approximately 3.14159), and \( r \) is the radius of the circle.</div> <div class="text">- **Explanation:** The formula represents a fraction of the entire circle area (\( \pi r^2 \)), where the fraction is determined by the central angle \( n \) out of the total possible angle of 360 degrees.</div> <div class="header">2. Finding Central Angle</div> <div class="text">- **Central Angle Formula:** To find the central angle \( n \) when given the sector area \( A \) and radius \( r \), rearrange the sector area formula as \( n = \frac{360A}{\pi r^2} \).</div> <div class="text">- **Application:** This variation is useful when you have the sector area and need to determine the central angle.</div> <div class="header">3. Finding Radius</div> <div class="text">- **Radius Formula:** If the sector area \( A \) and central angle \( n \) are known, the radius \( r \) can be found using \( r = \sqrt{\frac{A \times 360}{\pi n}} \).</div> <div class="text">- **Purpose:** This formula is handy when you have the sector area and central angle but need to find the radius.</div> <div class="header">4.

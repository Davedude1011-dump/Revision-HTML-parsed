Standard Form of the Equation</div> <div class="text">- **Formula:** The standard form of the equation of a circle with center coordinates (h, k) and radius 'r' is given by (x - h)² + (y - k)² = r².</div> <div class="text">- **Explanation:** In this form, (h, k) represents the center of the circle, and 'r' is the radius. The equation expresses the relationship that the distance from any point (x, y) on the circle to the center is equal to the radius.</div> <div class="header">2. General Form of the Equation</div> <div class="text">- **Formula:** The general form of the equation of a circle is x² + y² + Ax + By + C = 0, where A, B, and C are constants.</div> <div class="text">- **Conversion:** To convert the general form to standard form, completing the square on both the x and y terms is necessary.</div> <div class="header">3. Insights from the Equations</div> <div class="text">- **Radius and Center:** In both forms, the coordinates (h, k) represent the center, and 'r' is the radius. The standard form offers an immediate interpretation of these values.</div> <div class="text">- **Comparison:** The general form allows for a broader understanding of the circle's equation but may require additional steps for extracting information about the center and radius.</div> <div class="header">4.

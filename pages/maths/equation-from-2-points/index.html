The equation of a line, typically in slope-intercept form (y = mx + b), can be found using the following steps:</div> <div class="header">Step 1: Calculate the Slope (m)</div> <div class="text">The first step is to calculate the slope (m) of the line using the formula:</div> <div class="text">**m = (y₂ - y₁) / (x₂ - x₁)**</div> <div class="text">Where:</div> <div class="text">- (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂) are the coordinates of the two points.</div> <div class="text">Now, let's work through examples to understand this process:</div> <div class="header">Example 1</div> <div class="text">Given two points A(2, 3) and B(5, 7), find the equation of the line passing through these points.</div> <div class="text">Step 1: Calculate the slope (m)</div> <div class="text">**m = (7 - 3) / (5 - 2) = 4 / 3**</div> <div class="header">Step 2: Use the Point-Slope Form</div> <div class="text">Now that we have the slope (m), we can use the point-slope form of the equation:</div> <div class="text">**y - y₁ = m(x - x₁)**</div> <div class="text">Using point A(2, 3), we substitute the values:</div> <div class="text">**y - 3 = (4/3)(x - 2)**</div> <div class="header">Step 3: Simplify to Slope-Intercept Form</div> <div class="text">To express the equation in slope-intercept form (y = mx + b), we simplify the equation:</div> <div class="text">**y = (4/3)x - 2/3**</div> <div class="text">So, the equation of the line passing through points A(2, 3) and B(5, 7) is **y = (4/3)x - 2/3**.</div> <div class="header">Example 2</div> <div class="text">Given points C(-1, 2) and D(3, -4), find the equation of the line through these points.</div> <div class="text">Step 1: Calculate the slope (m)</div> <div class="text">**m = (-4 -

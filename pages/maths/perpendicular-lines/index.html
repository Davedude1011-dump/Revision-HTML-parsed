Right Angle Intersection: Perpendicular lines intersect at a 90-degree angle, forming four right angles at their point of intersection.</div> <div class="text">2. Negative Reciprocal Slopes: The slopes (gradients) of two perpendicular lines are negative reciprocals of each other. If one line has a slope of **m**, the other line's slope is **-1/m**.</div> <div class="text">3. Product of Slopes: If you multiply the slopes of two lines that are perpendicular to each other, the result is always **-1**. This is another way to express the relationship between their slopes.</div> <div class="header">Identifying Perpendicular Lines</div> <div class="text">To determine if two lines are perpendicular, you can use the following methods:</div> <div class="text">1. Use the Angle Criteria: If two lines intersect and form four right angles at the point of intersection, they are perpendicular.</div> <div class="text">3.

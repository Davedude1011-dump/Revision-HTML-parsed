Bearings are commonly used in navigation, engineering, and various applications where direction matters.</div> <div class="header">Rules for Bearings</div> <div class="text">To use bearings effectively, you should keep these rules in mind:</div> <div class="text">1. Bearings are measured clockwise from the north direction. North corresponds to 0 degrees, east to 90 degrees, south to 180 degrees, and west to 270 degrees.</div> <div class="text">2. Bearings are always expressed as three-digit angles. For example, 045° represents northeast, and 270° stands for due west.</div> <div class="text">3. When calculating bearings, make sure to measure the angle from the starting point (A) to the destination point (B) in a clockwise direction. This is the bearing of B from A.</div> <div class="text">4. Bearings are typically rounded to the nearest whole degree unless specified otherwise.</div> <div class="text">5. If the bearing is less than 100 degrees, it should be written with leading zeros. For example, 045° instead of 45°.</div> <div class="header">Calculating Bearings</div> <div class="text">To calculate the bearing of point B from point A, follow these steps:</div> <div class="text">1. Determine the direction from point A to point B using a compass or map. This direction is the angle measured clockwise from north.</div> <div class="text">2. Convert this angle to a three-digit bearing, rounding to the nearest whole degree if necessary.</div> <div class="text">3. Express the bearing as a three-digit angle, ensuring it has leading zeros if it's less than 100 degrees.</div> <div class="text">For example, if point B is located northeast of point A, the bearing of B from A would be 045°.</div> <div class="header">Applications of Bearings</div> <div class="text">Bearings find applications in various fields, including navigation, aviation, surveying, and engineering.

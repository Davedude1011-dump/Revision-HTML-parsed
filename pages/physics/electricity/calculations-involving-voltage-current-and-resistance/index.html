Ohm's Law and the power formula are essential tools for performing calculations involving these electrical parameters. Let's explore the key equations.</div> <div class="header">1. Ohm's Law</div> <div class="text">Ohm's Law establishes the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance in a circuit:</div> <div class="text">\[ V = I \cdot R \]</div> <div class="text">Where: <ul class="text"> <li>\(V\) is the voltage across the component in volts (V).</li> <li>\(I\) is the current flowing through the component in amperes (A).</li> <li>\(R\) is the resistance of the component in ohms (\(\Omega\)).</li> </ul> </div> <div class="header">2. Rearranging Ohm's Law</div> <div class="text">Ohm's Law can be rearranged to solve for current (\(I\)) or resistance (\(R\)):</div> <div class="text">\[ I = \frac{V}{R} \]</div> <div class="text">\[ R = \frac{V}{I} \]</div> <div class="header">3. Power Formula</div> <div class="text">The power (\(P\)) in a circuit can be calculated using the power formula:</div> <div class="text">\[ P = V \cdot I \]</div> <div class="text">This equation can also be expressed using Ohm's Law:</div> <div class="text">\[ P = I^2 \cdot R = \frac{V^2}{R} \]</div> <div class="header">4. Example</div> <div class="text">Consider a circuit with a voltage of 12V and a resistor with a resistance of 4 ohms.

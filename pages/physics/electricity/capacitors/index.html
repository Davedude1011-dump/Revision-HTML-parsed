They find applications in various devices, from filters to timing circuits. Let's delve into the basics of capacitors, relevant formulas, and common applications.</div> <div class="header">1. Capacitor Basics</div> <div class="text">A capacitor consists of two conductive plates separated by an insulating material known as a dielectric. When a voltage is applied across the plates, it creates an electric field, and the capacitor stores electric charge. Capacitance (\(C\)) is the measure of a capacitor's ability to store charge and is measured in farads (F).</div> <div class="header">2. Capacitance Formula</div> <div class="text">The formula for capacitance is given by:</div> <div class="text">\[ C = \frac{Q}{V} \]</div> <div class="text">Where: <ul class="text"> <li>\(C\) is capacitance in farads (F).</li> <li>\(Q\) is the charge stored on the capacitor in coulombs (C).</li> <li>\(V\) is the voltage across the capacitor in volts (V).</li> </ul> </div> <div class="header">3. Energy Stored in a Capacitor</div> <div class="text">The energy (\(U\)) stored in a capacitor can be calculated using:</div> <div class="text">\[ U = \frac{1}{2} C V^2 \]</div> <div class="header">4. Charging and Discharging</div> <div class="text">Capacitors can be charged and discharged through a resistor. The charging and discharging processes follow exponential curves given by the formulas:</div> <div class="text">\[ V(t) = V_0 \left(1 - e^{-\frac{t}{RC}}\right) \]</div> <div class="text">\[ V(t) = V_0 e^{-\frac{t}{RC}} \]</div> <div class="header">5. Applications</div> <div class="text">Capacitors have numerous applications, including:</div> <div class="text"> <ul class="text"> <li>- Energy storage in electronic devices.</li> <li>- Timing circuits in oscillators.</li> <li>- Filtering unwanted signals in electronic circuits.</li> <li>- Power factor correction in electrical systems.</li> </ul> </div> <div class="header">6.

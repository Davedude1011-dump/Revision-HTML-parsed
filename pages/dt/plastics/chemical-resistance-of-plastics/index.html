Polymer Composition</div> <div class="text">- **Chemical Structure:** The molecular structure of the polymer determines its resistance to different chemicals. Environmental Factors</div> <div class="text">- **Temperature:** Elevated temperatures can accelerate chemical reactions and increase the likelihood of chemical attack on plastics. Some polymers may exhibit reduced chemical resistance at higher temperatures, while others are specifically designed for high-temperature applications.</div> <div class="text">- **Exposure Duration:** Prolonged exposure to chemicals can compromise the integrity of plastics over time, leading to degradation, swelling, or loss of mechanical properties. Short-term exposure to certain chemicals may be tolerable, but continuous contact can cause deterioration.</div> <div class="header">3. Chemical Compatibility</div> <div class="text">- **Chemical Nature:** Plastics vary in their compatibility with different types of chemicals based on their chemical structures and properties. It's essential to consider the specific chemical composition, concentration, and temperature of the substances in contact with plastics to assess compatibility.</div> <div class="text">- **Testing and Evaluation:** Chemical resistance testing involves exposing plastic samples to various chemicals under controlled conditions to assess their performance and durability. Standardized test methods help quantify chemical resistance and predict material behavior in real-world applications.</div> <div class="header">4.
